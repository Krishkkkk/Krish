What is Magic?

Magic, also known as prestidigitation or close-up magic, is the art of performing tricks and illusions using manual dexterity and skillful manipulation of small objects like cards, coins, or other items. It involves techniques such as palming (concealing objects in the hand), switching (replacing one object with another), false shuffling or cutting (giving the illusion of mixing cards without altering their order), misdirection (distracting the audience from secret moves), and steals and loads (secretly taking or placing objects). Sleight of hand relies on precision, speed, and smooth execution to deceive the audience, often performed up close to create a personal and impressive magical experience.
